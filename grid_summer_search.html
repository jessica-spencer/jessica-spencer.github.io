Why Does Summer Search Matter?

How Our Alumni Outpace the Nation

At Summer Search, we believe in every young person's inherent strength and potential. For over 35 years, we've been breaking down systemic barriers that stand in the way of BIPOC and First-Generation students. Our focus has helped our alumni in the critical transitions from high school to post-secondary education and early career success.

Our students come from families that make less than $30,000 a year, which is under the NYC poverty line. We provide career-readiness workshops, mentorship, and financial wellness courses that help lead them on a path towards better educational, employment and financial futures.

Educational Attainment 1

Research shows that education is the key to escaping poverty. Our Alumni outperform the national average in educational attainment.

85% of Summer Search Alumni complete their Bachelor's Degree, versus only 21% of Low-Income First Generation students nationally.

Financial Well-Being

Our financial workshops and mentorship lay the groundwork for Summer Searchers to practice financial responsibility. Our goal is that they can fully meet current and ongoing financial obligations, can feel secure in their financial future and is able to make choices that allow them to enjoy life.

For example, in their group mentoring sessions, students participate in a budgeting exercisesb and discuss needs vs. wants in relation to spending money and achieving goals. Additionally, our High school seniors work with staff members to complete a cost assesment of different educational options and FAFSA forms, furthering their understanding of how to finance their chosen educational pathway.

80% of our students consistently have money left over at the end of each month

Employment Opportunity

Summer Search has a proven track record of working with organizations to provide apprenticeships.

One example is our partnership with Shawmut Design and Construction. Since 2005, Summer Search has matched Shawmut with 2-4 college age interns each year. Over the course of this partnership, Shawmut has had over 50 Summer Search intern

Our alumni used the opportunities given to go from intern to full-time hire.

70% of respondents surveyed were employed full-time 6 months after they completed their highest degree, compared to 53.6% of graduates nationally.

6% of Summer Searchers had an annual salary of $25,000 or more six months after they graduated with their highest degree; which is above the NYC poverty threshold for a family consisting of one person
