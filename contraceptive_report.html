Increasing Contraceptive Choice: Opportunities and Health Impacts in Indonesia

By Jessica Spencer | September 2024

Background and Significance

Contraceptive access is a key factor in improving health outcomes and economic opportunities. It helps reduce premature births, manage chronic conditions, and supports mental well-being.

Key Findings

The study, based on the 1987 Indonesian Contraceptive Prevalence Survey, found that education plays a crucial role in increasing contraceptive use among women.

Methodology

Using multiple logistic regression analysis, the study examined factors influencing contraceptive use, focusing on age, education, and socioeconomic status.

Results

  • Women with higher education levels were more likely to use long-term contraception.
  • Short-term contraception was more common among younger women.
  • The probability of not using contraception decreased with both age and education.

The graph above illustrates that as age increases, the preference for long-term contraception also rises, while short-term contraception usage declines.

The second graph demonstrates that as education increases, long-term contraceptive preference also increases, short-term use slightly increases, and the probability of not using contraception decreases.

Conclusion

Investing in women's education is a powerful strategy to improve contraceptive use and, consequently, reproductive health in Indonesia.
